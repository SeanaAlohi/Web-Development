About Me

Bio:

My name is Seana Marie 'Alohilani Lanias. I was born and raised on the island of Maui. I am currently attending the University of Rochester concentrating in Studio Art. I have been featured in university undergraduate exhibitions in Hartnett Gallery, Asis Gallery, Art and Music Library, and at Rennert's Gallery in Chelsea, NYC. I am a multi-racial woman of Native Hawaiian descent, which is one of the many topics I explore through my art. As an artist, I attempt to showcase my identity and my struggles stemming from this identity through mixed media painting, sculpture, printmaking, and performance.

Artist Statement:

Before taking my first art class in college, I thought an artist was someone who painted or drew realistically and beautifully. But as I progressed I was encouraged to take my art beyond this. I let my mind and body speak by applying paint to the canvas. I later found that I could do this with many mediums at once. I now mostly combine paint, rope, fabric, metal, natural elements, and beading to express the release of tension from my experiences throughout life. I like to explore parts of my identity that have impacted my experience in the world, such as being a woman and a mixed-race Native Hawaiian navigating prejudice in the United States for the first time since leaving Hawai'i.
